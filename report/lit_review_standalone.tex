\begin{abstract}
\noindent
This review establishes the probabilistic and computational
foundations required to solve for Nash equilibria, and to detect
tacit collusion, in the Cont--Xiong (2024) dealer market-making
game via deep BSDE methods for jump-diffusion processes.
Forty-two papers are organised across five sections --- BSDE and
mean-field game theory, the McKean--Vlasov FBSDE framework,
deep-learning numerics, empirical dealer-market applications, and
research frontier --- and synthesised under a single arc:
established knowledge, outstanding gaps, recent advances, and the
motivation for the contributions of this thesis.
\end{abstract}
